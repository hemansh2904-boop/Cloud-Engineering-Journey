# Internet

## Definition

The Internet is a global network of interconnected networks that enables communication and data exchange between devices worldwide.


## Characteristics

* Connects millions of networks across the world.
* Allows communication between hosts on different networks.
* Uses standard protocols such as TCP/IP.
* Enables services such as web browsing, email, cloud computing, and online communication.


## Examples

* Browsing YouTube
* Accessing AWS services
* Sending emails
* Video conferencing
* Online gaming


## Important Points

* The Internet is often called a "network of networks."
* A device does not need to be directly connected to another device to communicate through the Internet.
* The Internet enables global communication by connecting local, regional, and international networks.
* Most modern online services rely on the Internet.


## Interview Notes

**Q: What is the Internet?**
A: The Internet is a global network of interconnected networks that enables communication and data exchange between devices worldwide.

**Q: Why is the Internet called a network of networks?**
A: Because it is formed by connecting many smaller networks together, allowing devices on different networks to communicate.

**Q: Is the Internet a single network?**
A: No. The Internet consists of many interconnected local, regional, and global networks.
